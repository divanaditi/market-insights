Direct TPMS uses sensors placed in the tyres to measure the air pressure directly and transmits real-time data to the vehicle's onboard system. This type offers more accurate and precise measurements and alerts the driver when the pressure in any tyre drops below the recommended level. As a result, Direct TPMS is gaining preference among premium and high-performance vehicle manufacturers due to its reliability and advanced monitoring capabilities. Instead, it uses the vehicle’s wheel speed sensors to detect changes in tyre pressure by monitoring the rotation speed of the tyres. When a tyre's pressure drops, it affects the wheel's rotation, which is then detected by the system. Indirect TPMS is more cost-effective and easier to maintain, which makes it appealing for mass-market and budget-friendly vehicles. Though it may not offer the same level of precision as Direct TPMS, its lower installation and maintenance costs contribute to its widespread adoption.
