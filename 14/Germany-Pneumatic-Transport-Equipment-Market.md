This type of system is widely used for conveying bulk materials like powders and granular substances that require minimal product degradation and less air consumption. Dense phase systems are highly preferred in industries such as food processing, chemicals, and plastics, where product integrity is essential. Furthermore, these systems provide greater energy efficiency compared to their lean phase counterparts, making them a sustainable choice for many companies aiming to reduce operational costs and environmental impact.<p>On the other hand, lean phase pneumatic conveying systems operate by using high velocity and lower pressure to move materials. These systems are more suited for lighter materials, such as powders or small particles, that are less sensitive to abrasion or degradation. Lean phase systems are commonly used in industries like pharmaceuticals, cement, and agriculture. The simplicity of lean phase systems, combined with their ability to handle a wide range of material types, makes them a cost-effective and versatile option for many businesses.
