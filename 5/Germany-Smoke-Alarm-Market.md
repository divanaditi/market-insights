These alarms use a small amount of radioactive material to ionize the air inside the sensing chamber. When smoke particles enter, they disrupt the ionization process, triggering the alarm. Ionization smoke alarms are widely used in homes, offices, and industrial spaces for their fast response to fires involving flammable materials. Photoelectric smoke alarms are often considered more effective at detecting slow, smoldering fires, which are responsible for the majority of fire-related fatalities.
