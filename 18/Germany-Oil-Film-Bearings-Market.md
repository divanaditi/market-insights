Hydrodynamic bearings are widely used in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and manufacturing due to their high load-bearing capacity and durability. Their ability to operate without direct metal-to-metal contact contributes to longer service life and reduced wear and tear in machinery.<p>On the other hand, hydrostatic bearings operate on the principle of externally pressurized lubricant supplied to the bearing surfaces. This type does not require the relative motion between the bearing and the shaft to generate the lubricant film, allowing for precise control of the pressure and load distribution. Hydrostatic bearings are commonly used in high-precision machinery where minimal friction and accurate positioning are critical.
