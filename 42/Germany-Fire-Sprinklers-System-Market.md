These systems offer faster response times and are preferred for areas where freezing is not a concern. Dry pipe systems, on the other hand, are used in environments subject to low temperatures, as the pipes are filled with pressurized air rather than water. When a fire triggers the system, the air is released, allowing water to flow. Pre-action systems combine the features of both wet and dry pipe systems and are typically used in high-risk environments, providing an additional layer of protection. Deluge systems are used in areas that require large volumes of water in a short time to suppress a fire effectively.</p><p>The wet pipe sprinkler system leads the market due to its reliability and widespread application in residential, commercial, and industrial buildings.
