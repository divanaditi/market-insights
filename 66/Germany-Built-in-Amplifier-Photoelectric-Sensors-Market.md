Through-beam sensors consist of a transmitter and receiver that are placed opposite each other, creating an invisible beam. When the beam is interrupted by an object, the sensor detects the disruption and triggers a response. These sensors are highly reliable for long-distance detection and are often used in applications where objects need to be detected at a distance, such as in industrial automation systems and material handling processes. The retro-reflective sensors, on the other hand, use a single housing for both the emitter and receiver. The light emitted by the sensor is reflected back by a reflector, allowing the sensor to detect objects when they interrupt the light beam. This type is ideal for shorter distances and provides more cost-effective solutions for applications requiring simple object detection systems.</p><p>The market also features diffuse photoelectric sensors, which detect objects based on the reflected light from the surface of the object being detected.
